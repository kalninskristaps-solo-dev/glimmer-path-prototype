## 1. Narrative purpose

Story is **glue for progress and endowment**, not a novel.

| Job | How |
|-----|-----|
| Justify the map | Restoring the Glimmer Path region by region |
| Feed ownership | Each win visibly heals *your* grove |
| Support seasons | Rotating Dim manifestations + festivals |
| Reward collectors | Optional codex lore |
| Stay out of the way | ≤2 short lines pre-episode; skip always available |

**Players who ignore story still get a full puzzle game.**  
**Players who care get years of soft lore.**

---

## 2. Premise

Long ago, the **Glimmer Path** carried living light between every glade, river, and peak. The Path shattered. Light scattered into **motes**. Where motes fall out of harmony, **The Dim** seeps in — fog, hush, forgetting.

You are a **Pathkeeper**. By aligning motes, you teach light to remember its shape. Guide-spirit **Luma** (a lantern that grew a soul) walks with you.

You do not “kill candy monsters.” You **restore a world that becomes yours**.

## 3. Tone and themes

| Tone | Notes |
|------|--------|
| Hopeful cozy | Default |
| Light bittersweet | Loss of the old Path; recovery not instant |
| Adult-accessible | Not only for children; no crude edge |
| Low anxiety villain | The Dim is entropy/fear, not gore |

**Themes:** memory, caretaking, small daily acts matter, light as community.

**Refuse**

- Paywalled story chapters  
- Wall-of-text lore popups every level  
- Guilt plots about real-world friends/family  
- Dark “you abandoned your grove forever” permanent death

## 4. Characters

### Luma

- Role: tutor, cheer, gentle streak reminders.  
- Voice: warm, short, optional VO.  
- Arc: from flickering to steady as the Path returns.

### The Pathkeeper (player)

- Mostly silent avatar.  
- Named optionally in settings.  
- Titles in meta reflect years of play.

### The Dim

- Force of unmaking.  
- Seasonal faces (not always on-screen):  
  - **Mist Mother** (fog / film blockers)  
  - **Hollow Crown** (greed / locked riches imagery for economy events)  
  - **Storm Sleeper** (chaos boards, gravity flips)  
- Never requires hate-clicks every level; presence in key art and boss boards.

### Supporting (sparse)

- River warden, canopy elder, seasonal festival NPCs — 1 beat each, not a soap opera cast.

## 5. Campaign structure

### Chunking

- **Episode** = 15 levels + 1 story beat (CCS-proven size).  
- **Act** = group of episodes with biome + fantasy payoff.  
- **Season** = parallel or post-campaign content every 6–8 weeks.

### Act map (launch spine)

| Act | Episodes (example) | Biome | Story payoff |
|-----|--------------------|-------|--------------|
| **I — Awakening** | 1–5 (75 levels) | Home glade, first path | Meet Luma; first light; learn specials |
| **II — Root & River** | 6–15 | Roots, brooks, moss | Dim breach; first mold; community hint |
| **III — High Canopy** | 16–25 | Trees, wind bridges | Circle social unlock; Prism mastery |
| **IV — Broken Crown** | 26–30 | Shattered summit | Climactic Path stitch; Master Path unlock |
| **V — Seasons Eternal** | post | Rotating | Endless seasonal campaigns |

Exact level counts flexible; **Act I must feel complete** if soft-launch content is shorter.

### Side groves (optional branches)

- Unlocked by stars or episode clears.  
- 10–15 levels each.  
- Primary: **collection albums** + cosmetics.  
- Never required for main ending.

### Master Path

- Remix hard versions of beloved boards.  
- Narrative frame: “walk the Path again to keep it bright.”  
- Titles + frames; prestige without blocking new players.

## 6. Map fantasy (campaign UX)

```
[Dim mist] ---- [Glade*] ---- [River*] ---- [Canopy] ---- [Summit]
                  | side
               [Firefly hollow]
```

- Linear main path with readable next node.  
- Cleared nodes glow; uncleared sit in mist (art doc).  
- Player’s **Home Glade** always accessible for cosmetics.  
- Episode start: Luma one-liner + goal icons.

## 7. Story delivery formats

| Format | Length | When |
|--------|--------|------|
| Silent cinematic | 5–15s | Act open/close |
| Dialogue cards | 1–3 lines | Episode start |
| Board skin change | ambient | Mid-act |
| Codex entry | 50–80 words | Collection unlock |
| Season comic page | 4–6 panels | Season start/end |

**Skip** on all cinematics. Never make story a softlock.

---

## 8. Episode story beats (template)

**Episode N title** — poetic short name (“Moss That Remembers”)

1. **Hook line** (Luma or world).  
2. **15 levels** teaching/testing one gameplay idea.  
3. **Payoff** — grove prop lights; optional codex crumb.  
4. **Bridge** — one sentence toward next episode.

Boss every 5th episode: multi-phase Dim board + slightly longer win fanfare + map visual unlock.

---

## 9. Seasonal campaign model (long-term)

Every **6–8 weeks**:

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Narrative | One Dim face or festival (e.g. “Lantern Tide”) |
| Levels | 30–50 new / reskinned |
| Map dress | Seasonal palette + props |
| Track | Free + premium (hooks doc) |
| Codex | 5–10 new pages |
| Temporary mode | Event Cup board |

**Returning seasons:** yearly remix with quality-of-life + new album page so veterans still itch.

**Year arc (optional, light):** four seasons → “anniversary Pathwalk” title for active players.

## 11. Lore depth (optional codex)

Categories:

- **Path history** fragments  
- **Creatures of light**  
- **Dim manifestations**  
- **Pathkeeper orders** (titles explained)  
- **Seasonal festivals**  

Unlock via albums and milestones — **hooks document’s completion engine** with narrative flavor.

Tone of entries: field-notes, not encyclopedia dump.

## 12. Integration with addiction systems (honest mapping)

| Story surface | Hook it feeds |
|---------------|----------------|
| Grove healing | Endowment / daily return |
| “Path dimming” while away | Returner wilt (soft) |
| Season festivals | FOMO + identity |
| Codex missing pages | Zeigarnik |
| Pathkeeper titles | Status / long-term |
| Luma streak lines | Habit (gentle, not abusive) |

Story **must not** invent fake social obligations. Luma never says “your real friends hate you.”

---

## 13. Localization & content cost

- Minimal text, high reuse of templates.  
- Art-led storytelling reduces translation load.  
- Seasonal comics: 1 page each for cost control.  
- Names of episodes: short for UI.

---

## 14. Success criteria for story

- Soft-launch players can retell premise in one sentence.  
- ≥X% open codex once (telemetry later).  
- Skip rate high is OK; **grove attachment** is the real narrative KPI.  
- No support tickets about forced unskippable cutscenes.

---

## 15. One-line story promise

**You are not crushing candy for a king — you are keeping a living path alight, one honest match at a time, in a grove that remembers you came back.**
